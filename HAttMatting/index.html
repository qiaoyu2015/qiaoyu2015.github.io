Abstract

Existing deep learning based matting algorithms primarily resort to high-level semantic features to improve the overall structure of alpha mattes. However, we argue that advanced semantic features extracted from CNNs contribute unequally for alpha perception and we are supposed to reconcile advanced semantic information with low-level spatial cues to refine foreground details. In this paper, we propose an end-to-end Hierarchical Attention Matting Network (HAttMatting), which can predict exquisite structure of an alpha matte from single RGB image without additional input. Specifically, we employ spatial and channel-wise attention to integrate spatial cues and pyramidal features in an innovative fashion. This blended attention mechanism can perceive alpha mattes from refined boundaries and adaptive semantics respectively. We also introduce a hybrid loss fusing Structural SIMilarity (SSIM), Mean Square Error (MSE) and Adversarial loss to guide the network to further improve the overall foreground structure. Besides, we construct a large-scale image matting dataset comprised of 59,600 training images and 1000 test images (totally 646 individual foreground alpha), and they can further improve the robustness of our hierarchical structure aggregation. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed HAttMatting can capture sophisticated foreground structure and possess remarkable versatility, and achieve state-ofthe-art performance with single RGB image as input.
